Id Names::["id1_name","id2_name","id3_name"]

Description::"Description of part and numbering scheme"

Logic_ids::numbering logic in form of %10d number (X XXX XXX XXX with spaces separating the channel ids); channel ids used in logic (ordered)

Channel_ids::[id1: range, id2: range, id3: range]

Number of channels::number of channels of current part when DB is filled

  • NOTE #1: if z side is needed, assignment to logicId will be
    z side = -1 --> sideIndex = 0;
    z side = 1 --> sideIndex = 2;
  • NOTE #2: general idea for EE logicIds: a lower number (from 2 000 000 000 onwards) means that the part is bigger :)
    Hierarchy:
    TCC 1 XXX
    Whole Endcap 2 000 000 000
    Sides 2 000 000 01X
    Dees 2 000 000 1XX
    Sectors 2 000 000 XXX Note: dee and sector logicIds don't overlap - 1st of the 3 free digits is 0 or 2 for sectors (=sideIndex)
    DCC 2 000 001 XXX
    Crystal number 2 01X XXX XXX
    Crystal hashed 2 02X XXX XXX
    Readout tower 2 000 0XX XXX
    Trigger tower 2 000 0XX XXX
    Readout tower strip 2 001 XXX XXX
    Trigger tower strip 2 001 XXX XXX
    Readout tower xtal strip 2 00X XXX XXX Note: trigger/readout tower (and xtal) strip logicIds don't overlap - 1st of the 7 free digits is 1 for trigger (=TCC), 6 for readout (=DCC)
    Trigger tower xtal strip 2 00X XXX XXX
  • NOTE #3: ... = consecutive numbers
  • NOTE #4 ~..= almost consecutive numbers (e.g. crystal number for x,y)
